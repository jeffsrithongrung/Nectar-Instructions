Evaluate workflow

Rate, review, or diagnose existing creative. 1–10 scale across 4 dimensions plus a territory flag.

Scored dimensions

  • Hook strength — thumbstop + curiosity gap + demo fit
  • Sell sequence coherence — motivation → proof → CTA flow
  • Claim integrity — all claims pass constraint gate
  • Production quality — visual direction + shootability

Territory flag (not scored)

Known Territory — shares archetype + structure with existing assets.

New Territory — no close match. Triggers new Entity ID.

Brief workflow

Build production briefs. If creative direction is missing, Claude presents directions first (Creative Direction Gate).

1

Establish scope

Product line, variant count, testing structure, aspect ratios.

2

Creative Direction Gate

If unspecified, Claude presents 3–5 directions before building.

3

Get the brief

6 sections: Metadata, Strategy, Recipe Matrix, Component Tables, Tags, Entity ID Strategy.

4

Refine

Adjust components, swap headers, rewrite sales sequences.

Brief sections (from J437 / J359)

  • 1 — Metadata: Job name, owner, aspect ratios, base job, visual style, demo, VO, music
  • 2 — Strategy: SKU, motivator, funnel, testing goal, hypothesis
  • 3 — Recipe Matrix: Component combinations per variant and aspect ratio
  • 4 — Component Tables: Headers/Openers, Sales Sequences or Onramps
  • 5 — Tags: Standard mandatory tag set
  • 6 — Entity ID Strategy: Inherit or break per variant cluster

Testing structures

H×O×SS — Headers × Openers × Sales Sequences. For Structures A, E.

H×O×OR — Headers × Openers × Onramps. For Structures B, C.

Video breakdown with Gemini

Use this prompt in Gemini to break down video ads into structured data Claude can analyze.

Setup

1. Turn on thinking mode. In Gemini, enable "Think" or "Deep Think" for better transcription accuracy.

2. Paste the prompt below as the first message.

3. Upload up to 10 videos at once. Gemini will process them sequentially — completing each video's full table before moving to the next.

The prompt

Role: You are a Lead Creative Strategist specialized in direct-response video ads. Your task is to perform an Atomic Shot-by-Shot Breakdown of video assets to populate a creative testing library. Objective: Analyze the provided video(s) and produce a high-fidelity data table for each file. You must treat the video as a series of individual data points. Formatting Rules (Strict): Shot-by-Shot Precision: Every single visual change is a new row. This includes: - A camera angle change or cut. - A new text overlay appearing or disappearing. - A change in the subject's action (e.g.; switching from "holding box" to "opening box"). - Even 0.5-second "micro-cuts" must be logged separately. CSV Integrity: - Use semicolons (;) instead of commas in descriptions to prevent CSV breaking. - NO line breaks within cells. - Ensure descriptions are "Visual Anchors" — specific enough to distinguish between two different actors doing the same thing (e.g.; "Mustache man in grey shirt" vs "Woman in yellow jumpsuit"). Transcription: If the VO matches the On-Screen Text exactly; write "(Matches OCR)". If there is no text or no speech; write "[None]". Output Structure: For EACH video uploaded; provide: Header Data: - Asset Filename: [Name] - Estimated Duration: [X] seconds - Hook Archetype: [Choose: Skeptic/Honesty; Life Hack; Visual ASMR; Direct Address; Us vs. Them; etc.] - Primary Structure: [A (Narrative); B (Listicle); C (Rapid Proof); or D (Testimonial)] - Primary Motivator: [Problem; Outcome; Deal; or Anti-Alternative] The Data Table: | Timestamp | Visual Description | On-Screen Text (OCR) | VO Script | |---|---|---|---| | 00:00 | [Atomic detail with semicolons] | [Text] | [Script] | Processing Constraint: If multiple videos are uploaded; process them one by one. Complete the entire table and header for Video 1 before moving to Video 2. Analyze up to the 60-second mark.

After you have the breakdown

Paste it into the Nectar Claude project:

  • Scale: "ADAPT this ad into Bamboo" + paste
  • Analyze: "Decompose this — hook mechanism, structural engine, copy engine"
  • Compare: "How does this compare to Asset #26?"
  • Evaluate: "Rate this against our quality rubric"

Tips

Check the hook archetype tag. Gemini's classification is a starting point — verify before using in Claude.

If VO is missed, replay at 0.5x and correct manually.

For batch uploads, verify each breakdown individually. Accuracy can drift on videos 7–10.

IC tips

What to bring

Be specific. "NEW concept, Adjustable Bundle, Structure E, mid-funnel" beats "write me some ads."

Name the asset when scaling. "ADAPT #26 into Bamboo" beats "something like our plot twist ads."

Bring your hypothesis. "I think there's an angle around sleep tracking" gives Claude a grounded starting point.

Steering phrases

"Too safe"
Shifts to lower-saturation archetypes and white space.
"Too generic"
Adds specificity: numbers, named situations, viewer behaviors.
"More like #X"
Decomposes Asset X's mechanism and applies it.
"Not DR enough"
Tightens sell sequence, checks barrier-busting, adds urgency.
"Feels like an ad"
First-person voice, removes brand from first 10 seconds.
"Push harder"
Counter-intuitive angles, cultural moment hooks.
"Tighten to Xs"
Compresses — cuts filler, keeps strongest 2–3 proof points.

Working with outside ads

Claude can't watch video. Provide:

  • Option 1 — Scene breakdown (best). Use the Gemini prompt.
  • Option 2 — VO transcript. Claude analyzes from words alone.
  • Option 3 — Text description. Less precise but workable.

Auto-tagging

Every output includes: structure (A–E), hook archetype, lane(s), motivator, offer position, job type, funnel stage, duration. If tags are missing, ask Claude to re-tag.

What Claude won't do until asked

Visual direction — no shot lists until "format for production."

Full formatting — no timestamps until "format this."

Strategy essays — opens with directions or copy, not analysis.
